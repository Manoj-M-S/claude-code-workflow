# QED42 Positioning Reference

## Core positioning

QED42 is a digital experience company. We build and modernise digital platforms for
enterprises and mission-driven organisations that need to perform at scale and integrate AI
where it adds measurable value.

---

## What QED42 does better than anyone else

QED42 takes complex digital platforms that already matter to the business and makes them
perform better without losing operational reality.

That means improving speed, governance, usability, and scalability inside environments
where content, teams, integrations, and business dependencies are already complex.

Where this becomes especially distinctive: AI is introduced where it improves a measurable
workflow — search, publishing, structured content generation, decision support, operational
efficiency — while keeping governance and reliability intact.

QED42 is strongest when an organisation needs a digital platform to become faster, easier
to manage, and more valuable to users, without adding unnecessary complexity.

---

## Four core service areas

**1. Digital experience platforms and CMS modernisation**
Enterprise CMS strategy, content architecture, platform migration, governance, and
long-term maintainability across complex digital ecosystems.

**2. Drupal engineering at enterprise scale**
Large Drupal implementations involving multisite platforms, multilingual delivery, editorial
workflows, complex integrations, and platform consolidation. QED42 has 170+ contributions
to the Drupal community.

**3. JavaScript engineering, headless architecture, and modern frontend systems**
Decoupled architectures, headless builds (Next.js, Nuxt, React, React Native), and
JavaScript frameworks for greater frontend flexibility, performance, and multi-backend
integration.

**4. AI services built around practical business use cases**
Retrieval augmented generation, agent-based and multi-agent systems, AI-powered search,
document assistants, monitoring and evaluation, model adaptation, and controlled pilots
designed around real operational use cases. Performance-first, human-supervised,
context-aware, responsible AI principles.

## The buyer

**Most common titles:**
Chief Digital Officer, Chief Technology Officer, CIO, VP of Digital, Director of Digital
Experience, Head of Web Platforms. In mission-driven organisations: Director of Digital
Communications, Director of Technology.

In many enterprise deals, the executive sponsor is CXO-level but the active buyer is the
person directly below who owns platform outcomes, delivery pressure, and vendor
coordination.

**Their biggest headaches in 2026:**

1. **Legacy platforms are slowing business decisions.** Content takes too long to publish,
   integrations are brittle, too many vendors, platform changes carry operational risk.
   AI readiness is forcing technical debt conversations faster.

2. **AI pressure is high, but trust is low.** Asked internally what AI should do for them
   now, but pilots aren't scaling because governance, data quality, and measurable
   outcomes are unclear. They want practical AI tied to workflows, not experiments.

3. **Performance now affects business visibility directly.** Slow digital systems affect
   search visibility, customer experience, publishing speed, and internal confidence.

4. **They need one partner who understands both platform depth and execution reality.**
   Frustration with separate strategy firms, dev vendors, AI consultants, and design
   teams that don't connect operationally.

**The buyer is accountable for:** Improving digital outcomes without disrupting business
continuity, while proving new technology decisions will deliver value.
