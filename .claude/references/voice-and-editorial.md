# QED42 Voice and Editorial Reference

## Positioning in one sentence

QED42 builds and modernises digital platforms for enterprises and mission-driven
organisations that perform at scale and integrate AI where it adds measurable value.

---

## The Voice

QED42 sounds like a senior practitioner who has done this work, not a consultant who
describes it from the outside. The writing is direct, outcome-focused, and technically
grounded without being inaccessible.

Three qualities that define QED42 voice:

1. **Business-aware** — connected to organisational goals, publishing speed, governance,
   and decisions that cost real money or time
2. **Technically credible** — accurate enough that a Drupal architect or engineering lead
   nods along; not dumbed down for the sake of accessibility
3. **Human and accessible** — clear sentences, no jargon for jargon's sake, no filler

The single best model is William Zinsser's _On Writing Well_: strip every sentence to its
load-bearing parts. If a word doesn't carry weight, cut it.

---

## DO

- **Lead with the problem or the outcome.** The reader's question is "why does this matter
  to me?" Answer it in the first two sentences.
- **Use short paragraphs.** 2–4 sentences. One idea. White space is part of the voice.
- **Be specific.** "Release cycles shortened from days to hours" > "improved publishing
  speed." "580% more direct bookings in five months" > "significant growth."
- **Name the constraint.** The best QED42 writing acknowledges what made something hard:
  a legacy CMS, a fragmented multisite structure, regulatory publishing timelines, a tight
  budget, an audience with limited digital literacy. Constraints are where the thinking
  shows.
- **Credit the client's team.** QED42 works _alongside_ internal teams, not as a replacement.
  The goal is always that the client owns and runs the system after handoff.
- **State outcomes in the client's terms.** Not "we reduced technical debt" — "the team
  now ships content without raising a development request."
- **Use active voice.** The platform handles 500,000 daily visitors. The stock module ranks
  for high-volume keywords. The team publishes without external vendors.

---

## DON'T

**Language to avoid entirely:**

- "In today's digital landscape..."
- "As organisations increasingly..."
- "Leveraging [anything]"
- "Holistic approach"
- "Seamless experience" / "seamless integration"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Robust solution"
- "End-to-end" (unless describing a specific, concrete scope)
- "Excited to share" / "Thrilled to announce" / "Proud to partner with"
- "World-class"
- "Future-proof"
- "Delighted"
- "Cutting-edge"
- "Innovative" (if you have to say it, it isn't)
- "Game-changer"
- "Synergy" / "synergistic"
- "Value-add"
- "Unlock [potential/value/capabilities]"
- "Digital transformation journey" (transformation is fine; "journey" is not)
- "Move the needle"
- "At the end of the day"
- "Circle back"
- "Deep dive"

**Framing to avoid:**

- Staff augmentation framing ("we provide developers to your team")
- Features-first writing (capabilities before outcomes)
- Abstract AI promises ("AI will transform how you...")
- Vague impact claims ("significantly improved performance")
- Summary conclusions ("In conclusion, QED42's approach shows that...")
- Rhetorical questions as openers ("Have you ever wondered why...")
- Self-congratulatory framing ("As a leader in enterprise digital...")

## Sentence-level patterns from real QED42 writing

Study these patterns — they define the rhythm:

**Problem first, constraint named:**

> "Their public platform is a pre-login informational portal where prospective investors
> come to learn about products. As that audience grew, so did the demand on the platform."

**Decision explained plainly:**

> "Fixing everything at once on a platform this size wasn't an option. The portal served a
> large daily audience, and any disruption would be immediately visible."

**Outcome stated without inflation:**

> "Frontend effort is down by over 40%. The infrastructure handles over 500,000 daily
> visitors without latency or downtime."

**Constraint acknowledged honestly:**

> "Like most premium hospitality brands entering the direct-booking space, Banyan Living
> faced a structural challenge: the off-the-shelf tools available for short-term rental
> management are built for listings at scale, not for brands with a distinct voice."

**Client ownership emphasised:**

> "Technical choices were made around what the team could own and extend over time, not
> just what would work for the build."

**Impact in plain language:**

> "Content now ships ahead of market interest rather than after it."

## The Zinsser Test

Before any piece is final, apply this test to every paragraph:

1. Could any sentence be cut without losing meaning? Cut it.
2. Is every adjective doing work? If not, remove it.
3. Does the opening earn the reader's attention in two sentences or fewer?
4. Does the ending tell the reader what to do or think next — not summarise what you
   just said?

## Editorial philosophy

- **We reject staff augmentation framing.** QED42 is a digital experience company, not a
  body shop. Content never frames QED42's value as "providing capacity" or "extending
  your team with developers."
- **We lead with outcomes, not features.** The fact that QED42 uses Drupal, Next.js, or
  a particular AI framework is context. The outcome — faster publishing, fewer vendors,
  measurable performance — is the point.
- **We avoid generic B2B language.** If the sentence could appear in any digital agency's
  blog without changing a word, it doesn't belong in QED42's content.
- **We are honest about complexity.** QED42 doesn't oversimplify what enterprise digital
  transformation involves. The writing respects the reader's intelligence and experience.
- **AI is practical and governed, not aspirational.** QED42's AI work is introduced where
  it improves measurable workflows — search, publishing, content generation, decision
  support — with human supervision and governance. Never hype.
